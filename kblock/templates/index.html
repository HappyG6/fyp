About Us

What is blockchain technology?

Blockchain has a great reputation in the so-called digital currency environment of Bitcoin. Despite the Bitcoin hype, it is not the only useful application of blockchain technology.

However, to understand other applications, you must understand blockchain.

Simply put, it is a decentralized database that maintains a growing list or chain of data transaction records. Every part of this decentralized system has a copy of the chain of records; no "official" copy exists. In this way, the information is protected from alternating and rewriting.

  • Faster transaction settlements
  • Blockchain are time stamped
  • The transactions that take place are transparent. The individuals who are provided authority can view the transaction.

What We Can Provide

Enhanced security

By creating a record that cannot be altered and is encrypted end-to-end, blockchain helps prevent fraud and unauthorized activity.

Increased efficiency

Traditional paper processes are time-consuming. By streamlining these processes with blockchain, transactions can be completed faster.

Automation

Transactions can even be automated with “smart contracts”. Smart contracts reduce reliance on third parties to verify that terms of a contract have been met.

Instant traceability

Blockchain creates an audit trail that documents the provenance of an asset at every step on its journey.

Greater transparency

Blockchain uses distributed ledger technology, transactions and data are recorded identically in multiple locations.

Increased Convenience

Verifying and storing certificate here prevents losing it in the future and also easily to acquire whenever needed.

  • How blockchain works?

    As each transaction occurs, it is recorded as a “block” of data. Each block is connected to the ones before and after it. Transactions are blocked together in an irreversible chain: a blockchain.

  • What is Blockchain security?

    Blockchain security differs by blockchain types. Public blockchains are public, and anyone can join them and validate transactions.Public blockchains are public, and anyone can join them and validate transactions. Private blockchains are restricted and usually limited to business networks. A single entity, or consortium, controls membership. Permissionless blockchains have no restrictions on processors. Permissioned blockchains are limited to a select set of users who are granted identities using certificates.
